The Problem

Flicker is the quality tax on AI video.

Diffusion models generate each frame independently. Archival footage degrades unevenly over decades. The result is the same: luminance inconsistency that makes professional output unusable — and no existing tool fixes it without sacrificing perceptual quality.

⌇
AI-Generated Content
Diffusion models generate each frame independently. Without temporal awareness, the result is perceptible boiling — luminance values shifting frame-to-frame even in locked-off shots.
◫
Archival & Restoration
Decades of degradation manifest as irregular brightness shifts. Standard restoration tools smooth the problem away — along with the original detail that made the footage worth preserving.
▣
Existing Tools Fall Short
Standard temporal averaging trades one artefact for another — suppressing luminance variance at the cost of motion blur, ghosting, and loss of the fine detail that made the footage worth stabilising.
